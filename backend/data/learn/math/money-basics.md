## Canadian money

Coins and bills use **dollars** and **cents** (100 cents = $1). When you **estimate** costs, round to the nearest dollar or ten dollars to get a quick total.

## Making change & totals

- Add prices **step by step** or add dollars first, then cents.
- **7 packs at $2.85** → **7 × $2.85**. One way: **7 × $3 = $21**, then subtract **7 × $0.15 = $1.05** → about **$19.95** exactly if you multiply carefully.

## Budgets (big idea)

A **budget** lists **money in** and **money out** so you can plan. For word problems, underline **what you know** and **what you need to find**.

## Decimals and money

Money uses **two decimal places** (cents). **$2.85** is the same idea as **2.85** in decimal form.
