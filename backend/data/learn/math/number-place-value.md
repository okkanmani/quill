**Place value** means each digit’s worth depends on where it sits (ones, tens, hundreds, …).

Example: **4,582** = 4 thousands + 5 hundreds + 8 tens + 2 ones.

## Rounding

Rounding picks a **nearby** “easier” number, often to the nearest ten, hundred, or decimal place.

- Look at the digit **just to the right** of the place you’re rounding to.
- If it is **0–4**, keep the target digit the same.
- If it is **5–9**, **increase** the target digit by 1 (and carry if needed).

**Example:** Round **47.856** to the nearest **tenth** → look at the hundredths digit (**5**), so tenths go up: **47.9**.

## Comparing whole numbers

Line up by place value (or compare left to right). **3,412** is less than **3,501** because at the hundreds place, **4** is less than **5**.

## Estimation

Before you calculate, round numbers to make a **quick guess**. Then check whether your final answer is reasonable.
