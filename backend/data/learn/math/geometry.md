**Geometry** studies shapes, sizes, positions, and how they relate. Here are a few ideas you’ll see often in Grades 4–6.

## Points, lines, and angles

- A **point** marks a location (no size).
- A **line** goes on forever in both directions; a **line segment** has two endpoints.
- An **angle** is formed where two rays or segments meet. Angles are measured in **degrees (°)**.

Common angles:

- **Right angle:** **90°** (like the corner of a square)
- **Acute:** less than **90°**
- **Obtuse:** between **90°** and **180°**

## Triangles

- **Interior angles** of any triangle add to **180°**.
- Types by sides: **equilateral** (all sides equal), **isosceles** (two equal), **scalene** (none equal).
- Types by angles: **acute**, **right**, **obtuse**.

## Rectangles and squares

- **Perimeter** of a rectangle: **P = 2 × (length + width)**
- **Area** of a rectangle: **A = length × width**
- A **square** is a rectangle with all sides equal, so **A = side × side**.

## Parallelograms and trapezoids

- **Parallelogram:** opposite sides parallel and equal; area **A = base × height** (height is perpendicular to the base).
- **Trapezoid:** one pair of parallel sides (in many curricula). Area **A = ½ × (base₁ + base₂) × height**.

## Circles (intro)

- **Radius (r):** center to edge  
- **Diameter:** **2 × r**  
- **Circumference** (distance around): often **C ≈ π × diameter** or **2 × π × r**  
- **Area:** **A ≈ π × r²**

Use **π ≈ 3.14** unless your teacher says otherwise.

## Volume (simple solids)

- **Rectangular prism (box):** **V = length × width × height**
