## Types of graphs

- **Bar graph:** Compare **categories** (e.g. books read per month). Bar **height** or **length** shows the amount.
- **Pictograph:** Uses **symbols**; check the **key** (one symbol might mean 5 items).
- **Line graph:** Often shows **change over time** (temperature, height).

## Reading a graph

- Read the **title** and **axis labels** (what each axis measures).
- Use **grid lines** to estimate values when needed.

## Misleading graphs (good to notice)

- Axes that **don’t start at zero** can make differences look bigger.
- **Uneven scales** or **3D effects** can distort comparisons.

## Mean (average)

**Mean** = **sum of values ÷ how many values**.  
Backpack masses **2.1, 2.4, 2.0, 2.5** kg → sum **9.0**, count **4** → mean **9.0 ÷ 4 = 2.25** kg.
