**Geometry** studies shapes, sizes, positions, and how they relate. Diagrams and cut‑outs at home help a lot.

## Points, lines, and angles

- A **point** marks a location.
- A **line segment** has two **endpoints**; an **angle** is where two segments or rays meet.
- Angles are measured in **degrees (°)**.

Common types:

- **Right angle:** **90°** (square corner)
- **Acute:** less than **90°**
- **Obtuse:** between **90°** and **180°**

## Triangles

- **Interior angles** add to **180°**.
- By sides: **equilateral** (all equal), **isosceles** (two equal), **scalene** (none equal).
- By angles: **acute**, **right**, **obtuse**.

## Quadrilaterals (four sides)

- **Rectangle:** four right angles; opposite sides equal.
- **Square:** rectangle with **all sides** equal.
- **Parallelogram:** opposite sides parallel and equal.
- **Trapezoid:** (in many programs) **one pair** of parallel sides.

## Triangles and missing angles

In an **isosceles** triangle with two **50°** angles, the third is **180° − 50° − 50° = 80°**.
