A **fraction** shows parts of a whole. The number on top is the **numerator** (how many parts you have). The number on the bottom is the **denominator** (how many equal parts the whole is split into).

Example: **3/4** means 3 parts out of 4 equal parts.

## Equivalent fractions

Different fractions can name the same amount. Multiply or divide **both** the numerator and denominator by the same number (except zero).

| Fraction | One step | Equivalent |
|----------|----------|------------|
| 1/2 | ×2 on top and bottom | **2/4** |
| 2/3 | ×3 on top and bottom | **6/9** |

## Adding and subtracting

- **Same denominator:** Add or subtract the numerators; keep the denominator.  
  **2/7 + 3/7 = 5/7**

- **Different denominators:** Find a **common denominator** (often the least common multiple), rewrite each fraction, then add or subtract.

## Multiplying

Multiply numerators together and denominators together:

**2/3 × 4/5 = 8/15**

## Dividing

Multiply by the **reciprocal** of the second fraction (flip it):

**2/3 ÷ 4/5 = 2/3 × 5/4 = 10/12 = 5/6** (simplify when you can)

## Mixed numbers

A **mixed number** has a whole part and a fraction, e.g. **2 1/3**. For operations, it often helps to turn it into an **improper fraction**: **2 1/3 = 7/3**.
