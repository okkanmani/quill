**Decimals** are another way to write numbers “between” whole numbers, using place value with **tenths**, **hundredths**, and so on.

- **0.3** = three tenths = **3/10**
- **0.25** = twenty-five hundredths = **25/100** = **1/4** when simplified

## Place value (quick refresher)

| Ones | . | Tenths | Hundredths | Thousandths |
|------|---|--------|------------|-------------|
| 0 | . | 6 | 2 | 5 |

The number **0.625** is “six tenths, two hundredths, five thousandths.”

## Comparing decimals

Line up the decimal points and compare from left to right:

- **0.7** vs **0.65** → **0.70** is greater than **0.65** (same as comparing 70 hundredths to 65 hundredths).

## Adding and subtracting

Line up decimal points, then add or subtract as usual:

```
  12.40
+  3.08
--------
  15.48
```

## Multiplying

Multiply as if there were no decimals, then count **total** decimal places in the factors and put that many in the product.

**0.2 × 0.3 = 0.06** (one + one = two decimal places)

## Dividing

You can **shift** the decimal point in both numbers the same way to make the divisor a whole number, then divide.

Example: **4.5 ÷ 0.15** → multiply both by 100 → **450 ÷ 15 = 30**

## Rounding

Pick a place (e.g. hundredths), look at the next digit to the right: if it is 5 or more, round up; otherwise leave the digit as is.

**3.147** to hundredths → **3.15**
